## Unit 1 — Introduction & Elements of Language

### 课程定位

| 概念 | 定义 |

## Unit 2 — How Writing Came About (书写是如何产生的)

### 两大理论对比

| 理论 | 提出者 | 核心论点 |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Pictographic Theory** (象形理论) | William Warburton (1698–1779) | 最早的书写是用图形来描绘事物形象 → "images of things" |
| **Token Theory** (陶筹理论) | Leo Oppenheim (1959); Denise Schmandt-Besserat (1992) | 美索不达米亚人用陶筹记录货物 → 陶筹封印在泥板上 → 泥板压印 → 最终代替陶筹 → 书写诞生 |

### 关键概念
- **Rebus Principle** (画谜原则)：用代表声音的图画来拼出词语，如 "eye bee leaf" = "I believe"
- **Acrophonic Principle** (截头音原则)：一个符号获得其物体名称的第一个音位的音值
- **三种书写起源模式**：
  1. 只用 Rebus → 苏美尔语、中文
  2. Rebus + Acrophony → 埃及语、安纳托利亚象形文字
  3. 只用 Acrophony → 玛雅文、原始西奈字母

### 中文书写系统特点
- 从象形文字 (pictogram: sign → object) 到语标文字 (logogram: sign → word)
- 约 90% 的汉字是 **形声字 (semantic-phonetic compounds)** — 一部分表音 (rebus)，一部分表意 (radical 部首)

## Unit 3 — How Writing Represents Language (书写如何表现语言)

### 核心论点：真正的书写系统必须包含语音信息
- "Universal" 符号系统（不依赖语言的纯意义符号）始终是有限的
- **True writing = can express anything that can be expressed in spoken language**
- **True writing systems always contain significant amounts of phonological information**

### Signifier / Signified (索绪尔 Saussure)
- **Arbitrary** (任意性)：能指与所指之间没有必然联系
- **Conventional** (约定俗成性)：一旦被公众接受，个人不能随意更改

### 书写系统分类

```
Writing Systems
├── Logographic (语标文字) — 代表语素/词，如中文
│   └── 例: 汉字, Sumerian, Egyptian hieroglyphs
└── Phonographic (表音文字)
    ├── Syllabic (音节文字) — 代表音节，如日文假名
    └── Segmental (音段文字)
        ├── Abjad (辅音文字) — 只写辅音，如阿拉伯文、希伯来文
        ├── Alphabet (全音素文字) — 同时有辅音和元音，如希腊字母、拉丁字母
        └── Abugida (元音附标文字) — 元音通过附加符号表示，如天城文 Devanagari
```

## Unit 4 — Decipherment of Egyptian Hieroglyphs (埃及象形文字的破译)

### 核心故事：Young vs Champollion

| 人物 | 背景 | 方法 | 局限 |
|------|------|------|------|
| **Thomas Young** (1773–1829) | 物理学家、通才（光波理论、杨氏模量） | 推定 "Ptolemy" 在埃及文中是拼音拼写 | 认为拼音只用于外来人名，其余仍是表意文字 |
| **Jean-François Champollion** (1790–1832) | 语言学家、自幼学习科普特语 | **结构分析**：对比希腊文 486 词 vs 1419 个象形符号 → 不可能全是语标 → 也不可能全是表音字 → **混合系统** | 工作条件艰苦（依赖副本） |

### 破译方法论
1. **罗塞塔石碑 (Rosetta Stone, 1799)** — 同一内容用三种文字书写：希腊文、埃及世俗体 (Demotic)、象形文字 (Hieroglyphic)
2. **结构性推理** (Champollion)：符号总数 1419 → 若全是语标应有约 486 个词 → 若全是字母应有 20–30 种 → 166 种符号 → 两者都不是 → **混合系统**
3. **Held-out data (验证集)**：
   - **Philae Obelisk** (菲莱方尖碑) — 含有 Cleopatra 的名字 → 验证 Ptolemy 的拼读
   - **Ramesses 法老名** — 确认音标 + 语标的混合模式

### 核心结论
- 埃及象形文字 = **ideographic (表意) + phonetic (表音) 的混合系统**
- **Decipherment** = 确定书写与语言之间的关系 (Language + Script 两个要素)

## Unit 6 — Language, Writing, and Literacy in Society (语言、书写与社会素养)

### Literacy 的多维度定义
- **UNESCO (2004)**：15 岁以上能理解性读写简短日常生活陈述
- **UNESCO (2018/2026)**：不仅是读写算，更包括数字技能、媒体素养、可持续发展教育

### Literate Society (有文字的社会)
- 有书写系统的社会
- 历史上，**literati = created from the top** → 书写作为特权的标志
- 古埃及、美索不达米亚、迈锡尼、古代中国：训练精英书吏阶层，抵制文字简化

### 大辩论：Lévi-Strauss vs Goody

| 学者 | 主要观点 |
|------|---------|
| **Claude Lévi-Strauss** (结构人类学家) | 口述文化与文字文化差异不大；新石器时代（无文字）是人类最有创造力的时期之一；文字的主要功能是**便利奴隶制 (facilitate slavery)** |
| **Jack Goody** (社会人类学家) | 口述和文字都是线性的，但**文字的空间维度带来新自由度** → 列表和表格是口语中不存在的 → 强迫有序思维 |

### Alphabetic Literacy vs Logographic Literacy 论争
- **Moorhouse / De Kerckhove**：字母表促进分析性思维
- **William Hannas (2003)**：东亚表意文字体系抑制创造力（争议性观点）
- **反驳**：
  - 韩文 Hangul 是音段文字
  - 中文形声字读者可以通过声旁推断读音
  - 字母编码了声音但不编码语义深度（"photosynthesis" 能读不一定能懂）

### 中国的文字改革
- **罗马化尝试**：Wade-Giles → 邮政系统 → 注音符号 → 国语罗马字 → 拉丁化新文字 → **Pinyin**
- **简化字 (1956)**：减少笔画、采用简化偏旁和草书形式

## Unit 8 — Typewriter, Mind, and Style (打字机、思维与风格)

### 打字机的诞生
| 发明 | 时间 | 人物 |
|------|------|------|
| Hansen's Writing Ball (Skrivekugle) | 1870s | Rasmus Malling-Hansen |
| Sholes Typewriter | 1873 | Christopher Latham Sholes |
| Remington No.1 (商业化) | 1874 | Sholes + Remington |

### QWERTY 键盘的起源之争
- **传统观点**：为防止打字机卡键，按键频次高的字母被分开布置 (Jamming theory)
- **Yasuoka & Yasuoka (2011)**：QWERTY 是为 **Morse 接收员**设计的，后被电传打字机 (Teletype) 采用

### 键盘大战：QWERTY vs Dvorak
- Dvorak Simplified Keyboard (August Dvorak, 1936)
- 但 QWERTY 因 **network effect / path dependence** 延续至今

### 中文打字机（对数书写系统的技术挑战）
- Logographic vs Phonographic 的差别带来了技术难题
- 1880s–1940s 各种设计：Sheffield（4662字）、周厚坤（部首）、舒振东、**Qi Xuan（可拆分组合型）**、**林语堂 Mingkuai（明快打字机，以笔画为分类依据）**
- **关键 insight**：中文输入是一种 **retrieval-composition**（检索-组合），而非西方的 inscription-composition（铭写-组合）

## Unit 9 — Telegraph, Morse Code, and Global Connectivity (电报、摩斯密码与全球连接)

### 电报的发展
- Cooke & Wheatstone (英国，5 线电报)
- Morse & Vail (美国，电磁电报)

### Morse Code 的符号学架构
- 点 (dot) 和 划 (dash) 的二进制逻辑 — **predates binary in computer programming**
- SOS = `··· --- ···`
- 与 punched card (打孔卡) 共享概念相似性

### 对新闻写作的深远影响
- **Pay-per-word model** → 语言必须简短
- **Wire services (通讯社)**：AP, Reuters → 标准化、统一化新闻风格
- **Inverted Pyramid Structure (倒金字塔结构)**：最重要的信息放最前面
- **Headline Writing**：简短但信息密集

### 传播理论：James Carey's Ritual View
- 通信不仅是信息的空间传播，更是 **temporal dimension (时间维度) 的扩展**
- 通信技术构建共同体的仪式性过程

### 新媒体时代的批判性思考
- **Objectivity 的双面性**：作为商业技术（标准化、商品化）vs 作为职业伦理（中立、公正）
- **Self-media (自媒体)**：草根赋权 vs 缺乏编辑标准和事实核查
- **Filter Bubble / Echo Chamber / Information Cocoon**：算法加剧信息窄化
- **Critical Discourse Analysis (CDA)**：识别权力关系、意识形态、说服技巧、框架、互文性

## Unit 10 — Linguistics, Rationalism, and Rule-based MT (理性主义与基于规则的机器翻译)

### 形式主义 vs 功能主义
- **Formalism (形式主义)**：Saussure 结构语言学 → Chomsky 生成语法
  - 语言是明确定义的系统
  - 核心任务：描述 "ideal speaker-hearer" 的 **competence**
  - **Phrase Structure Rules (短语结构规则)**：S → NP VP
  - **Transformational rules (转换规则)**：主动 → 被动

### 规则机器翻译 (RBMT) 的三种类型 (Vauquois Triangle)

```
             Meaning (Interlingua)
                / \
               /   \
   Analysis   /     \   Synthesis
             / Transfer \
            /     |      \
         Word   Phrase   Word-based
       (Direct)  (Transfer)
```

| 类型 | 描述 | 举例 |
|------|------|------|
| **Direct (直接翻译)** | 逐词翻译 + 简单重排规则 | Mark II (俄-英, 1964) |
| **Transfer (转换法)** | 分析SL → 转换句法树 → 生成TL | 需要源语言分析器 + 翻译词典 + 转换规则 |
| **Interlingua (中间语言)** | 语言无关的语义表示 → 任一目标语言 | 概念优雅但难实现 |

### RBMT 的局限性
- 许多语言现象无法形式化
- 语言学家对规则有不同看法
- 长距离依赖 (long-distance dependency)、句法歧义、指代消解等
- → **导致停滞期 (Era of stagnation)**

## Unit 11 — Corpora, Empiricism, and Data-driven MT (语料库、经验主义与数据驱动机器翻译)

### 理性主义 vs 经验主义的钟摆
- **1950s: Empiricism** (Shannon, Skinner, Firth, Harris)
- **1970s: Rationalism** (Chomsky, Minsky)
- **1990s: Empiricism 回归** (统计 NLP)

### 语料库 (Corpus) 的定义
> 一个 **machine-readable**、**authentic**、**sampled to be representative** 的文本集合

- 单语语料库 → **Fluency (流畅度)**
- 双语/平行语料库 → **Adequacy (充分性)**

### 统计机器翻译 (SMT)
- **核心思想**：从平行语料库中学习翻译参数，不依赖语言学知识
- **IBM Models 1–5**：从简单到复杂的词对齐概率模型
- **SMT 基本公式**：P(e|f) ∝ P(e) × P(f|e) → 语言模型 × 翻译模型

### SMT 的发展脉络
```
Word-based SMT → Phrase-based SMT → Syntax-based SMT → Hybrid MT → Neural MT
```

| 阶段 | 特点 | 局限 |
|------|------|------|
| Word-based (IBM Models) | 逐词翻译 + 对齐概率 | 忽略多词单位、习语、局部语境 |
| Phrase-based (PBSMT) | 连续词组作为翻译单元 + 重排序模型 | 短语不一定有语言学依据 |
| Syntax-based (SBSMT) | 引入句法树指导翻译 | 需要树库 |
| Hybrid MT | 结合各方法优势 | 架构复杂 |
| **Neural MT (NMT)** | 非线性、多层、attention 机制 | 需要大量数据和计算资源 |

### NMT 的核心突破
- 引入 **latent representation (隐藏层)** 和 **attention weights (注意力权重)**
- 能更好地处理上下文和长距离依赖
- 非线性变换使得源语言 → 目标语言的映射更灵活

## Unit 15 — Social Media Era: Language Reconceptualized (社交媒体时代：语言的重新概念化)

### 核心问题：线上语言是第三种语言？
- 早期研究：pro-written / pro-spoken / hybrid 三种观点
- **Current consensus (Crystal 2011)**：口语和书写是多维连续体的两端，网络语言处于其中的不同位置
- 维度包括：synchrony (同步性), audience, style, ephemerality, anonymity, multimodality

### CMC (Computer-Mediated Communication) 的语言特征
- 英语：acronyms (LOL, GTG), word reductions (gd, hv), letter/number homophones (U, 2), stylized spelling, emoticons, unconventional punctuation
- 中文：类似特征 — 缩写 (npy), 数字谐音 (7456), 新旧词转换, 表情符号等

### 网络语言创造方式
| 方式 | 英文例 | 中文例 |
|------|--------|--------|
| Conversion (转类) | Google it, friend/unfriend | 百度一下、微信我、凡尔赛 |
| Compounds (复合) | homepage, broadband | 登录、私聊 |
| Derivation (派生) | cyber love, e-book, hyperlink | 网友、网民 |
| Old word + new meaning | bookmark, flaming, troll | 潜水、冒泡、喷子 |

### 括号标注 (Bracketed Annotation) 作为社交语用资源
- **核心发现**：在 CMC 中，括号从传统的书面标点符号 → **pragmatization**（语用化）：成为高度情感性、关系性、上下文敏感的交互工具
- **Cooperative Principle (Grice, 1975)**：括号标注帮助消除 emoji 的歧义
- **Politeness Principle (Leech, 1983)**：括号在维护对方面子中起作用
- **Digital Stage Whisper**：括号作为「数字舞台耳语」，允许人在不脱离前台风范的情况下传递后台信息

### 身份理论 (Identity)
- **Dramaturgical Theory (Goffman, 1959)**：社交生活如同剧场 — Front stage vs Back stage
- **Turkle (2011)**：我们上线是为了"做自己"，但线上表现有了自己的生命 → multiple selves
- **Parasocial relationship (准社会关系)**：单方面亲密感

### 关于网络语言的争论
- **Baron**：网络语言是威胁，可能导致标准语言"危险"，错误的"石化" (fossilization of errors)
- **Trendy but transient**：许多网络新词来得快去得也快（帅呆了、雷人、神马都是浮云、顶、萌、Hold住）

> **命题方向**：从书写诞生到打字机到数字媒介，书写技术如何重塑了人类社会？写作不仅仅是记录工具，它改变了什么？

### 📝 大作文 (300词) 结构模板

**Title:** *How Writing Technologies Have Reshaped Human Cognition and Society*

**Para 1 — Introduction (≈50词)**
Thesis: Writing is far more than a passive recording tool — as argued throughout this course, each writing technology has fundamentally reshaped how humans think, organize knowledge, and structure society.

**Para 2 — Writing systems and cognition (≈80词)**
Goody's counterargument to Lévi-Strauss demonstrates that the spatial dimension of text — lists, tables, ordered argumentation — "forces ordered thinking about phenomena" in ways that speech alone cannot. This insight is reinforced by the Chinese typewriter: as Unit 8 shows, Chinese input is a **retrieval-composition** process, a fundamentally different cognitive pathway from the **inscription-composition** of alphabetic typing.

**Para 3 — Technology and social structure (≈80词)**
Writing technologies also shaped social hierarchy. Unit 6 describes how ancient civilizations trained elite scribes who guarded literacy as a marker of privilege. Even in modern times, China's civil service examination "promoted scholarly literacy among a small elite group of males but hindered the spread of functional literacy among the masses." Telegraph technology similarly reshaped journalism: the pay-per-word model and wire services gave rise to the **inverted pyramid structure**, prioritizing information efficiency over narrative style.

**Para 4 — Contemporary implications (≈60词)**
Today, social media blurs the line between speech and writing. Crystal (2011) proposes that online language exists on a **multidimensional continuum** between spoken and written modes — a development that, as Baron warns, may lead to the "fossilization of errors."

**Para 5 — Conclusion (≈30词)**
In sum, from token to tweet, writing technologies do not merely record language — they actively reshape cognition, social hierarchy, and communicative norms.

### 📝 小作文 (100词) 结构模板

**Title:** *How the Telegraph Changed News Writing*

The telegraph's **pay-per-word model** transformed journalism. As Unit 9 explains, **wire services** (AP, Reuters) demanded brevity, giving rise to the **inverted pyramid structure**: the most critical information placed first. This created a "new objectivity" — both as a commercial technique (standardized, commoditized) and as professional ethics (neutral, impartial). The telegraph demonstrates how communication technology reshapes not just the speed of information transmission, but the very form and style of written expression.

## 话题二：Machine Translation — From Rules to Data to Neural Networks

> **命题方向**：机器翻译的发展历程反映了理性主义与经验主义的钟摆。两种范式各自的优势与局限是什么？NMT 是终极方案吗？

### 可用素材

| 可用素材 | Unit | 关键引用 |
|----------|------|---------|
| Formalism & Chomsky | U10 | "formalization of descriptions of linguistic systems" — phrase structure rules, transformations |
| RBMT limitations | U10 | "Many linguistic phenomena cannot be formalized" → era of stagnation |
| Corpus definition | U11 | "machine-readable, authentic, sampled to be representative" |
| Pendulum swings | U11 | "Rationalism vs Empiricism" — 1950s/1970s/1990s |
| IBM Models | U11 | Word-based SMT, phrase-based, syntax-based |
| NMT breakthrough | U11 | "Nonlinear, multiple layers, attention mechanism... Handle context way better!" |
| Interlingua concept | U10 | "a single, universal semantic representation" — elegant but problematic |

### 📝 大作文 (300词) 结构模板

**Title:** *From Rationalism to Empiricism: The Evolving Paradigm of Machine Translation*

**Para 1 — Introduction (≈50词)**
The history of machine translation reflects a pendulum swing between two competing paradigms: **rationalism**, which treats language as a formal system of rules, and **empiricism**, which learns from data. This course demonstrates that neither approach alone has proven sufficient.

**Para 2 — The rationalist approach (≈80词)**
Rule-based MT (RBMT), grounded in Chomskyan formalism, sought to encode linguistic knowledge through **phrase structure rules**, **transformational rules**, and, at its most ambitious, a language-independent **interlingua**. However, as Unit 10 documents, many linguistic phenomena "cannot be formalized," linguists disagree on rules, and challenges like **long-distance dependency** proved intractable — leading to an era of stagnation.

**Para 3 — The empirical turn (≈80词)**
The 1990s witnessed a revival of empiricism, driven by the availability of corpora and the insight that translation parameters could be learned automatically from **parallel corpora**. Starting from **IBM Models** (word-based alignment probabilities) and progressing to **phrase-based** and **syntax-based** SMT, these systems required no explicit linguistic rules. Yet they remained limited: word-based models "ignore multi-word units, idioms, and local context."

**Para 4 — Neural MT and beyond (≈60词)**
**Neural MT** represents a synthesis: its **attention mechanism** and **latent representations** allow it to "handle context way better" than earlier approaches. Yet the interlingua dream — elegantly captured by the Vauquois triangle — remains elusive, as different languages conceptualize the world differently (German's two words for "wall," Chinese's two words for "brother").

**Para 5 — Conclusion (≈30词)**
Ultimately, the history of MT suggests that progress comes not from rigid adherence to one paradigm, but from understanding language as both a formal system and a statistical phenomenon.

### 📝 小作文 (100词) 结构模板

**Title:** *Why Did Rule-Based Machine Translation Stagnate?*

Rule-based MT faced fundamental limitations (Unit 10). First, formalizing linguistic rules proved difficult: "many linguistic phenomena cannot be formalized," and linguists' views differ. Second, challenges such as **syntactic ambiguity**, **reference resolution**, and **long-distance dependency** required more than phrase structure rules could provide. As a result, by the late 1980s, RBMT had reached an "era of stagnation." This paved the way for the empirical turn: rather than hand-crafting rules, researchers began learning translation parameters automatically from **parallel corpora**, marking the rise of statistical MT.

### 📝 大作文 (300词) 结构模板

**Title:** *Language in the Age of Social Media — Decline or Creative Evolution?*

**Para 1 — Introduction (≈50词)**
Social media has sparked intense debate: is it corrupting language or creatively expanding it? Drawing on the course material, this essay argues that online language represents not decline but a **creative reconceptualization** of how we communicate and construct identity.

**Para 2 — Features of CMC (≈80词)**
Unit 15 documents characteristic features of **computer-mediated communication (CMC)**: acronyms and initialisms (LOL, GTG), word reductions (gd, hv), letter/number homophones (U, 2), and stylized spelling. In Chinese social media, parallel phenomena appear — number homophones (7456 for 气死我了), new compounds (网红), and conversions (百度一下). Crystal (2011) argues that these should not be viewed as a single "third medium" but as positions on a **multidimensional continuum** between speech and writing.

**Para 3 — Beyond mere novelty (≈80词)**
These linguistic innovations are more than transient fads. As the course reveals, bracket usage in WeChat group chats has undergone **pragmatization** — a shift from a traditional punctuation device to a rich interpersonal resource. Through the lens of Goffman's **dramaturgical theory**, brackets function as a **"digital stage whisper"**: a means of conveying backstage information while maintaining front-stage performance. This demonstrates that CMC users are not simply being lazy with language — they are creatively expanding its pragmatic repertoire.

**Para 4 — Concerns and counterpoints (≈60词)**
Baron raises legitimate concerns: habitual online language may lead to the **fossilization of errors** and push "standard language towards danger." However, as Unit 9 also reminds us, fears about new communication technologies are historically recurrent. The telegraph was once accused of destroying thoughtful prose, yet it gave us the **inverted pyramid** — a lasting contribution to informational efficiency.

**Para 5 — Conclusion (≈30词)**
Rather than lamenting decline, we should recognize that social media is reconceptualizing language in ways that reflect our evolving social and technological realities.

### 📝 小作文 (100词) 结构模板

**Title:** *How Social Media Shapes Online Identity*

Drawing on Goffman's **dramaturgical theory**, social media can be understood as a stage where users perform identity. The profile, avatar, and nicknames constitute **front-stage** self-presentation. Through bracketed annotations, users engage in **"digital stage whispers"** — conveying backstage emotion without fully breaking character. As Turkle (2011) observes, "our online performances take lives of their own," creating **multiple selves** of varying intensities. Social media thus blurs the neat boundary between front and back stage, offering new tools for identity construction while raising questions about authenticity.

## 话题四 Bonus：口述 vs 文字文化 — Lévi-Strauss 与 Goody 的辩论

> **备用话题**：如果考到关于 literacy 的本质、文字社会与口述社会的对比

**小作文参考 (100词)**：

The debate between Lévi-Strauss and Goody centers on whether writing fundamentally transforms cognition. Lévi-Strauss argued that literate and oral cultures "are not so different," noting that the most creative period of human history — the Neolithic — was pre-literate. He controversially suggested that writing's primary function was "to facilitate slavery." Goody countered that text's **spatial dimension** introduces a "new degree of freedom": lists and tables, absent in speech, "force ordered thinking about phenomena." This course suggests that while Lévi-Strauss was right to challenge technological determinism, Goody's argument better accounts for how writing reorganizes thought.
